Early history

In 1802, Humphry Davy used a high-powered battery to create an incandescent light by passing current through a thin strip of platinum. It was not bright enough nor long-lasting enough for practical use, but it was the precursor to the light bulbs we know today.

Commercial success

Joseph Swan and Thomas Edison are both credited with the invention of the first commercially successful incandescent light bulb around 1879. Edison's version used a carbonized bamboo filament that could last over 1,200 hours.

Modern innovations

Today, light bulbs have evolved into highly efficient LEDs (Light Emitting Diodes). Lumina continues this tradition by integrating smart technology, allowing for millions of colors and energy efficiency that Edison could only dream of.
